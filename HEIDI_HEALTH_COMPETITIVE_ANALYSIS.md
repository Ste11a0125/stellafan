# Heidi Health — Competitive Analysis

*For job application context. February 2026.*

---

## TL;DR for Your Interview

Heidi is a strong company to join, but go in with eyes open. They're the **fastest-growing bottom-up player** in a market dominated by enterprise giants. Their $65M Series B at $465M valuation is solid but modest compared to Abridge ($5.3B) and Ambience ($1B+). The opportunity: Heidi owns the individual clinician segment that enterprise players ignore. The risk: EHR integration depth is their Achilles' heel, and the market may consolidate toward enterprise-first platforms.

## 2. The Market

### AI Ambient Clinical Documentation — $600M+ and growing

This is healthcare AI's first breakout category. In 2025:
- AI-enabled healthcare startups raised 83% more per round than non-AI healthcare companies
- 100% of surveyed health systems report some ambient scribe usage
- Healthcare AI spending nearly tripled to $1.4B
- 85% of generative AI spend in healthcare flows to startups, not incumbents

### Market Share (Menlo Ventures, Oct 2024)

| Player | Market Share | Segment |

| Suki | 10% | Mid-market |

| Other (includes Heidi) | 7% | Mixed |

**Key insight:** The top two players control 63% of the market. Heidi is not yet a named player in US market share reports, though its 2M+ weekly consults globally suggest significant volume outside the US enterprise segment.

## 3. Competitive Landscape

### Tier 1: The Enterprise Giants

#### Microsoft Nuance DAX Copilot
- **Market share:** 33%
- **Advantage:** Deployed in 77% of US hospitals. Deep Epic integration. Microsoft distribution machine.
- **Weakness:** Expensive. Slow to innovate. Tied to existing hospital IT contracts.
- **Threat to Heidi:** Low for individual clinicians (DAX doesn't serve them). High if hospitals standardize on one platform.

#### Abridge
- **ARR:** ~$100M (estimated May 2025)
- **Valuation:** $5.3B
- **Total Funding:** $550M+ (Series D + E in 2025 alone)
- **Advantage:** Gave Epic an equity stake = 3-6 month integration lead. "Linked evidence" — click any note line to hear the source audio. Enterprise-only with deep EHR integration.
- **Weakness:** Enterprise-only. No free tier. Individual clinicians can't sign up. ~$500/month per clinician.
- **Threat to Heidi:** High in the enterprise segment. Low in bottom-up adoption.

#### Ambience Healthcare
- **ARR:** ~$30M (May 2025)
- **Valuation:** ~$1B
- **Total Funding:** $243M+ (Series C)
- **Advantage:** Building an "AI operating system" beyond just scribing — coding, workflows, patient comms. Backed by a16z + OpenAI fund.
- **Weakness:** Enterprise-only. Long implementation cycles. Less EHR integration depth than Abridge.
- **Threat to Heidi:** Medium. Competes more with Abridge than Heidi.

### Tier 2: The Mid-Market & Individual Players

#### Freed AI
- **Segment:** Individual clinicians and small practices
- **Advantage:** Easy to use. Affordable. No EHR integration required (copy-paste workflow).
- **Weakness:** Limited integration. Less sophisticated AI.
- **Threat to Heidi:** High — directly competes for the same bottom-up, individual clinician segment.

#### Suki
- **Market share:** 10%
- **Advantage:** Voice-command digital assistant (not just a scribe). Can pull patient info, dictate letters.
- **Weakness:** Narrower scope than full ambient documentation.
- **Threat to Heidi:** Medium.

#### DeepScribe
- **Segment:** Specialty care (oncology focus)
- **Advantage:** Deep specialty workflows. "Ambient OS" positioning.
- **Weakness:** Requires IT support. Niche focus.
- **Threat to Heidi:** Low — different positioning.

## 4. Heidi's Competitive Position

### What Heidi Does Well (Strengths)

| Strength | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Bottom-up adoption (freemium)** | Clinicians sign up without IT approval. This is how Slack, Figma, and Notion won. |
| **110+ languages, 116 countries** | No competitor has this geographic breadth. Enterprise players are US-focused. |
| **Speed of adoption** | 81M consults in 18 months. 2M+ weekly. This growth rate is extraordinary for a company this size. |
| **Doctor-founded** | Dr. Thomas Kelly brings clinical credibility. New CMO Dr. Simon Kos (ex-Microsoft) adds enterprise health cred. |
| **Low cost of acquisition** | Freemium means users self-serve. Compare to Abridge's enterprise sales cycle. |
| **Moving beyond scribing** | New AI agent that calls patients on behalf of doctors. Expanding the product surface. |

### Where Heidi Is Vulnerable (Weaknesses)

| Weakness | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **EHR integration depth** | Copy-paste workflow for many users. Abridge's Epic equity deal is a structural moat Heidi can't easily match. |
| **Not in US market share rankings** | Despite global volume, Heidi isn't a named player in the US enterprise market. |
| **Revenue vs. competitors** | ~$22M ARR vs. Abridge's ~$100M. The revenue gap is 4-5x. |
| **Funding gap** | $97M total vs. Abridge's $550M+ in 2025 alone. Capital disadvantage for enterprise sales buildout. |
| **Free tier economics** | High user count, but conversion to paid is the key question. Free users don't build a business. |
| **Australia-first** | The largest healthcare AI market is the US. Being Australia-based means playing catch-up on US enterprise relationships. |

### Assessment

**The round is respectable but not in the top tier for this market.** Here's the honest breakdown:

**Positive signals:**
- Point72 (Steve Cohen's fund) as lead — serious, sophisticated investor
- $465M valuation is strong for an Australian healthtech company
- 21x ARR multiple is healthy and suggests investors believe in growth trajectory
- New CRO from Plaid and CMO from Microsoft signal the company is building for enterprise scale

**Cautionary signals:**
- Abridge raised **8.5x more** in 2025 alone ($550M vs. $65M)
- Ambience raised **3.7x more** in their latest round
- The funding gap means Heidi cannot win a capital-intensive enterprise sales war against Abridge
- Average Series B in AI healthcare was $55M in 2025 — Heidi is just above average

**What this means for you as a potential employee:**
- Heidi has enough runway to execute (18-24 months at current burn)
- But they need to either (a) win on a different axis than enterprise sales, or (b) raise a significantly larger Series C
- The equity could be very valuable if Heidi finds a path to the enterprise or gets acquired, but the outcome range is wide

## 6. Strategic Analysis: Where Can Heidi Win?

### Per April Dunford: "What do we have to position against?"

Heidi's real competition isn't Abridge. It's the **status quo for individual clinicians**: hand-typing notes after hours, using basic dictation tools, or hiring human scribes at $25-50/hour. 40% of clinician time is spent on documentation.

### The Two Possible Strategies

#### Strategy A: Win Bottom-Up, Then Expand Up (The Figma Path)

```
Individual clinicians adopt free → Teams adopt paid →
Hospital notices 40% of doctors using Heidi →
Hospital signs enterprise contract
```

**Precedent:** Figma, Slack, Notion all won this way. Heidi's freemium model and 2M weekly consults suggest this is the current strategy.

**Risk:** EHR integration is the moat in healthcare. Hospitals won't sign enterprise deals without deep Epic/Cerner integration. Abridge literally gave Epic equity to lock this in.

#### Strategy B: Own the Long Tail (The Shopify Path)

```
Own individual clinicians and small practices globally →
Become the default for the 80% of clinicians who will
never be served by enterprise solutions
```

**Precedent:** Shopify won by serving the millions of small merchants that Salesforce/Oracle would never touch.

**Risk:** Lower revenue per user. Harder to build a massive business on $50-100/month subscriptions vs. Abridge's $500/month enterprise contracts.

### My Assessment

Heidi's best path is **Strategy B with a bridge to A**. Own the individual clinician globally (where they already have extraordinary traction), build EHR integrations incrementally, and let bottom-up adoption create enterprise pull. The 116-country footprint is a genuine moat — Abridge and Ambience are US-focused and will be slow to internationalize.

## 7. What to Ask in Your Interview

These questions will show you've done your homework and are thinking strategically:

1. **"What's the current paid conversion rate from the free tier, and how is it trending?"** — This is the single most important business metric for Heidi's model.

2. **"How are you thinking about EHR integration strategy relative to Abridge's Epic partnership?"** — Shows you understand the structural challenge.

3. **"Is the long-term strategy to win enterprise deals or to own the individual clinician segment globally?"** — Tests whether leadership has a clear strategic direction.

4. **"What's the path to $100M ARR, and how much of that comes from the US vs. international?"** — Shows you're thinking about the business, not just the product.

5. **"How does the new AI patient-calling agent fit into the product vision — is Heidi becoming a platform or staying focused on documentation?"** — Tests whether they're building coherently or feature-chasing.

## 8. Bottom Line: Should You Join?

| Factor | Signal | Rating |
|---|---|---|
| Market | Massive, growing, proven demand | Strong |
| Product | Loved by users, 2M+ weekly consults | Strong |
| Funding | Solid but outgunned by Abridge/Ambience | Moderate |
| Competitive position | Owns a segment leaders ignore | Moderate-Strong |
| Growth trajectory | 81M consults in 18 months is exceptional | Strong |
| Risk | EHR integration gap; US market penetration | Moderate |
| Upside | If bottom-up works, this is Figma for healthcare | High |

**Verdict:** Heidi is a good company to join if you believe in the bottom-up, international strategy. The risk is real — they're outfunded by competitors with deeper enterprise moats. But the user adoption velocity is genuinely impressive, and the individual clinician segment is large and underserved. If they execute the Figma/Slack playbook in healthcare, the equity outcome could be very strong.

The biggest question to resolve in your interview: **does leadership have a clear, credible path from "lots of free users" to "lots of paying enterprise customers"?** If the answer is convincing, join. If it's hand-wavy, proceed with caution.
